Mid-Word Ga3ya with Shewa

The combination of ga3ya with shewa (MAM, KCT, SBB) rarely appears like this, in the middle of a word. It is briefly mentioned in section 335 of Yeivin’s Introduction to the Tiberian Masorah. I find that this is one of eight cases in Tanakh. The other seven are shown below. They are shown as they appear in MAM.

Pr 8:34 אַ֥שְֽׁרֵי־אָדָם֮
Ps 1:1 אַ֥שְֽׁרֵי־הָאִ֗ישׁ
Ps 40:5 אַ֥שְֽׁרֵי־הַגֶּ֗בֶר
Ps 98:9 לִ֥פְֽנֵי־יְהֹוָ֗ה
Ps 64:7 יַ֥חְפְּֽשׂוּ־עוֹלֹ֗ת
Ps 89:25 וֶ֥אֱֽמוּנָתִ֣י ḥataf
Ps 98:3 וֶ֥אֱֽמוּנָתוֹ֮ ḥataf

Above, the last two cases are marked “ḥataf ” because the ga3ya with shewa is a ga3ya with a ḥataf shewa, i.e. a ga3ya with a (vocal) shewa notated as a ḥataf vowel (in this case, notated as a ḥataf segol ). So, they look a little different than their counterparts notated with a simple shewa. But this difference is cosmetic, i.e. they are still quite strictly analogous. Those last two cases also look a little different because they involve a simple rather than compound word. Again, this difference is cosmetic, i.e. they are still quite strictly analogous.
