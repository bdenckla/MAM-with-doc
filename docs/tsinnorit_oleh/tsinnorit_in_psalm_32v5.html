Tsinnorit in Psalm 32:5 ועוני

What accents, if any, can appear on a non-initial vocal shewa? The answer is: none, except for a misplaced tsinnorit that is seen in Psalm 32:5 ועוני in some editions. In these editions, the tsinnorit of ועוני appears on its ayin (ע) whereas it should appear on its second vav (ו):

wrong וַעֲ֘וֺנִ֤י
right וַעֲוֺ֘נִ֤י

The editions having this error include these:

  • Mechon Mamre
  • The Complete Tanach with Rashi

(Both of the above editions also have a gaʿya on the first vav, but that is a legitimate variant, though it reflects they more gaʿya-heavy style associated with the printed tradition rather than the tradition of the most authoritative manuscripts.)

The correctly-accented version of this word is typographically challenging, particularly with the “tilde” form of tsinnorit. (This “tilde” form is the form shown above.) It is challenging because the tsinnorit contends for space with a ḥolam ḥaser dot on a narrow letter, namely, vav.

(For info about how BHS meets these typographic challenges, see my document, “Tsinnorit and the Ḥolam Ḥaser dot in BHS.”)
