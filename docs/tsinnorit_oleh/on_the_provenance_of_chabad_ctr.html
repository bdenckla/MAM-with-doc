On the Provenance of Chabad’s CTR

The Chabad website has an edition of the Hebrew Bible called The Complete Tanach with Rashi (CTR). (This edition also goes by other similar names such as The Complete Jewish Bible with Rashi Commentary.) I suspect that Chabad’s CTR is derived from a CD-ROM version of CTR that was a collaboration between Judaica Press and the software company Davka. (See, e.g., the record for this CD-ROM at the NLI.)

I suspect that the CD-ROM was itself derived from the digital sources used to create the Prophets and Writings put out by Judaica Press. (One problem with my theory is that Judaica Press does not have a full Torah: only Genesis and Exodus.)

I also suspect that all of these Judaica Press products, both paper and CD-ROM, have some relation to Soncino products. In particular, I suspect that the Judaica Press Prophets and Writings has some relationship to the second edition of the Soncino Books of the Bible. And, I suspect that the CTR CD-ROM has some relationship to the Hebrew Bible found on the CD-ROM known as the Soncino Classics Collection.
